      <h1>Pillar IV: Integration of Magic &amp; Science</h1>
      <h2>The Reconciliation of What Was Never Truly Separate</h2>

      <div class="section">
        <h3>Core Principle</h3>
        <p>This pillar is our refusal to accept the false divide between the mystical and the measurable. Magic and science are not rivals — they are twin languages of the same truth. Science is the map. Magic is the compass. Together, they orient us toward wisdom. Integration means refusing reductionism: not stripping spirit from matter, nor matter from spirit, but seeing both as sacred expressions of the cosmos.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="section">
        <h3>Key Commitments</h3>
        <h4>No More False Binaries</h4>
        <ul>
          <li>Reject the cultural lie that says: choose spirit or science.</li>
          <li>Hold that data without wonder is soulless, and ritual without knowledge is rootless.</li>
          <li>Live in synthesis, not division.</li>
        </ul>

        <h4>Magic as Technology</h4>
        <ul>
          <li>Recognize that ancient rituals were technologies of survival, healing, and transcendence.</li>
          <li>Affirm that modern science often re-discovers what ancestors already knew through intuition and observation.</li>
          <li>Honor both laboratory and altar, telescope and tarot, microscope and medicine bag.</li>
        </ul>

        <h4>Curiosity as Sacred Practice</h4>
        <ul>
          <li>Hold experimentation, exploration, and discovery as spiritual disciplines.</li>
          <li>Treat questions as prayers, and knowledge as a form of devotion.</li>
          <li>Teach that skepticism without humility becomes arrogance, but curiosity with reverence becomes wisdom.</li>
        </ul>

        <h4>Applied Integration</h4>
        <ul>
          <li>Do not use science to disprove magic, nor magic to ignore science.</li>
          <li>Weave quantum physics into mystical teachings, biology into ritual, astronomy into ceremony.</li>
          <li>Teach members how to hold both equations and invocations in the same hand.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

      <div class="section">
        <h3>Practical Actions</h3>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>The Athenaeum Curriculum:</strong> Courses that pair mystical traditions with scientific insights (e.g., lunar rituals with astronomy; herbalism with botany and pharmacology).</li>
          <li><strong>Experiments in the Sacred:</strong> Ritual spaces that invite both measurable outcomes and mystical intention.</li>
          <li><strong>Sacred Labs:</strong> Spaces where members can test, track, and refine magical practices as living sciences.</li>
          <li><strong>Annual Symposium:</strong> Gathering where healers, scientists, mystics, and researchers present side by side, modeling integration.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

      <div class="section manifesto muted">
        <h3>Manifesto Declarations</h3>
        <p>We will not choose between microscope and moonlight.<br>
        We will not exile spirit from science or science from spirit.<br>
        We will not accept the severing of body and soul, matter and mystery.<br>
        We will study the stars with telescopes and with myth.<br>
        We will heal with medicine and with magic.<br>
        We will treat curiosity as sacred.<br>
        We will remember: integration is wholeness, and wholeness is power.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="section">
        <h3>Framework Beneath the Manifesto</h3>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Synthesis:</strong> Reject binaries. Hold both ways of knowing in harmony.</li>
          <li><strong>Technology:</strong> See rituals as ancestral technologies; see science as rediscovery of ancient wisdom.</li>
          <li><strong>Curiosity:</strong> Inquiry is not a threat to faith but its highest form.</li>
          <li><strong>Application:</strong> Mysticism and science are both made practical — for healing, for knowledge, for liberation.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
